SB205 Stormwater Discharge Compliance Form

Effective January 1, 2020 Senate Bill 205 requires a person or entity who conducts a business operation that is a “regulated industry” to demonstrate enrollment with the NPDES permit program. By providing specified information on an initial or renewal County business license application, enrollment in the NPDES permit can be confirmed. The goal of the program is to address the health and safety of drinking water sources statewide.

SB 205 does not affect all businesses. Only those businesses with primary Standard Industrial Classification (SIC) codes that are identified by the State Water Board as requiring enrollment in the Industrial General Permit (IGP) are affected. The questions below will guide you and the County in determining if your business requires enrollment in the IGP. Completion and submission of this form is required with your 2020 business license application/renewal.

1 What is the physical location/address of this business? Complete a separate SB205 Compliance Form for each physical location/address in the unincorporated area of Sacramento County.
2 Describe the nature of business being conducted at this business location.
3 Has the business already obtained an Industrial NPDES Permit? Please answer “Yes” or “No”
4 If you answered YES in Question #3, provide the requested information regarding the existing Industrial NPDES Permit. Sign the back of the page and return this form with your business license application or renewal application. You are in compliance with SB205.
